Navraj Narula

My name is Navraj Narula and I am an undergraduate student at Boston University majoring in English Literature, Secondary Education, and Computer Science. I enjoy writing creatively on multiple levels, given my various academic fields of interest. Yet I constantly find myself to be most enamored with syntax as it relates to programming languages.

For that reason, I am choosing to apply to graduate programs in computer science/engineering. I am most interested in database design and data analytics. In my spare time, I create relational databases and develop front-end tools to access them for the purpose of information retrieval. I am also fascinated by algorithms, particular those pertaining to graph theory and how they can be implemented to solve problems. My research portfolio is also documented on this page.

Deciding to pursue my passion mid-junior year has led me to this conclusion: Nothing has to remain confusing. I have made the greatest decision of all: to continue to study what I love! I am happily exploring classes, research areas, and events pertaining to computer science. My curiosity encourages me to ask questions, re-read, and despite all barriers: push through.
